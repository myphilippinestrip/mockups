A letter, to begin

You have been to the Philippines. Most likely, you went to Palawan; most likely, you loved it. You told your friends about the limestone and the water, and you have been waiting — quietly, these past two or three years — for the right excuse to come back.

Our job is not to introduce you to the Philippines. It is to introduce you to the Philippines you haven't seen yet.

— The editors · April 2026

Heritage · houses that open by appointment

Iloilo

Kansi sour enough to flinch at. Calle Real at 6:40. A grandson who answers the phone on the third ring.
02 / 08
Wonder · hills before the coaches

Bohol

The Chocolate Hills at 5:40 in the morning are a different country to the ones at noon. We go at 5:40.
03 / 08
Exuberance · a city built on sugar

Bacolod

Ancestral houses along the old Negros roads, MassKara in October, the right kind of late-afternoon light the rest of the year.
04 / 08
Gentleness · a boulevard at 6:40

Dumaguete
& Siquijor

Four days because three isn't enough. A ferry at 7. A balete tree that has been holding its ground since before the Spanish.
05 / 08
Ease · what the surfers do off the board

Siargao

Go for the surf and you miss Siargao. Go for what the surfers do on their rest days.
06 / 08
Vertical · limestone up, limestone down

Coron

Walls that go up and down in equal measure. Lagoons on their own clock. Snorkelling that ruins you for elsewhere.
07 / 08
Origin · the first cross, the first everything

Cebu

Heritage-led and unhurried. Oslob is off the itinerary, for reasons we are happy to explain.
08 / 08
Restoration · the quieter island, after

Boracay

Not the comeback you have already heard about, but the quieter thing that happened after. Station One at the hours most visitors miss.
