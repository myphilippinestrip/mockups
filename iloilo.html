Heritage · 3D2N · Iloilo

Iloilo, quietly.

Three days in a city that does not raise its voice. The heritage houses open by appointment, and appointment means phoning a grandson. The kansi in La Paz is bone-marrow sour. The churches are older than most of Europe thinks they are. This is the Philippines most Palawan travellers have not seen yet.

Iloilo rewards slowness. This is a heritage city, one of the oldest in the country, and its pleasures are the kind you find by walking, eating, and asking. Nothing on this trip is rushed. Nothing is big-ticket. The work of the three days is to let the place land.

You stay in the old downtown, walk Calle Real most mornings, and eat at the market at least twice. A guide who knows the heritage-house families will open doors that are not on any public itinerary. Sunday morning is for Molo or Jaro, whichever feels right that week.

The trip is private throughout. Your vehicle, your guide, your pace. There are no groups, no optional add-ons at the desk, no surprise upsells. The package is the package.

A morning in La Paz

The best morning of the week for kansi is Tuesday, because the bones come in fresh on Monday evening and the pot has had the right amount of time. The soup is famously sour, batuan fruit does the work, and travellers who have only had Manila versions tend to flinch at the first spoonful and then, within a minute, understand. Batchoy at Ted’s afterwards, if there’s room.

Calle Real, on foot

The old main street runs about a kilometre. Most of the façades are restored now, some are not, which is arguably better. A good guide will stop at three or four specific buildings and tell you which grandson to phone for interior access. Casa Mariquit is the usual one. It still opens on request.

Molo or Jaro, on Sunday

Molo is the one people call the feminist church. Every saint in the nave is a woman, which is unusual enough to have its own small scholarship. Jaro is the grander building and the patron saint’s home. Pick one, arrive before the 9am mass, and stay for it whether or not you are Catholic. The singing is the reason.

The days

  1. Day 01

    Arrival

    Landing at Iloilo International. Private transfer to your heritage hotel downtown. Afternoon free to settle in, walk Calle Real, or rest depending on flight time. Dinner on your own, with your guide’s recommendations in hand.

  2. Day 02

    Heritage, market, table

    Morning walk of Calle Real with your guide, Casa Mariquit included if it is open that day. La Paz market for kansi or batchoy, whichever your guide thinks is strongest that week. Afternoon at Molo church and plaza. Evening free.

  3. Day 03

    Church, and onward

    Sunday morning at Molo or Jaro, including the 9am mass. A late breakfast nearby. Return transfer to Iloilo airport for your afternoon flight out.
