Vertical / Limestone water

Coron

You have seen the photographs. You may even have taken some yourself, in El Nido, four hours south. Coron is not those photographs. Coron is the lakes inside the islands, and the climb to reach them.

Coron and El Nido are often discussed as the same trip, which they aren’t. They share a coastline and a kind of limestone, and that is roughly where the resemblance ends. El Nido is open water, beach-fronted lagoons, the long bangka tour. Coron is closer to interior. The famous places, Kayangan Lake, Barracuda Lake, the Twin Lagoon’s hidden inner basin, are inside the islands rather than around them. To reach them you climb over the rock. The water you swim in afterwards is still and freshwater, or stratified salt-and-fresh, and the colour is something the open ocean does not produce.

The trip is a single full day on the water, the Coron Ultimate Tour C, run by an accredited guide on a private bangka. You leave Coron Town early and reach Kayangan Lake before the day boats from the larger resorts arrive. From there: Twin Lagoon, Siete Pecados snorkelling, the Skeleton Wreck (a Japanese gunboat from 1944), CYC Beach, lunch on White Beach. Back to town by mid-afternoon. The sequence varies if the wind shifts; the guide reads conditions and rearranges.

After Palawan once, the question is whether Coron is repetition or extension. Our argument is extension. The colour is the same family; the geometry is genuinely different. The lakes are unlike anything in El Nido. If you want a second Palawan trip that shows you something new rather than the same thing again, this is the one.

Kayangan, the climb in

Kayangan Lake is reached by water and then by ladder. The boat ties up at a cliff, you walk a short path, and then you climb wooden steps over a limestone ridge, about three hundred steps depending on which side of the ridge you start. From the top there is a viewing platform, which is the photograph everyone takes; below it, on the descent, there is the lake itself. The water is freshwater, fed by springs in the rock, and is repeatedly described as the cleanest lake in the Philippines. Whether or not that is technically true, it is the cleanest-looking lake we have seen.

Twin Lagoon, where the wall opens

Twin Lagoon is two lagoons separated by a limestone wall. At low tide you can swim under the wall through a small opening, perhaps a metre and a half above the waterline; at high tide there is a wooden ladder over the top. The inner lagoon is smaller, quieter, and warmer in places where freshwater meets salt; the temperature changes are unsettling the first time you encounter them. You will encounter them. The thermocline is the point of the place.

The wreck at Skeleton

The Skeleton Wreck is a Japanese gunboat from the September 1944 American air raid that sank twelve ships in Coron Bay in a single afternoon. It rests in five to twenty-two metres of water; the upper deck is reachable from the surface with a snorkel and a held breath, and the rest is for divers. From above, the outline of a ship is the kind of thing that makes you go quiet. Coral has worked on it for eighty-two years. It is a war grave and a reef at the same time, which is one of those Pacific facts that takes a while to settle.

The days

  1. Day 01

    Arrival

    Landing at Busuanga airport, transfer to Coron Town. Around an hour by van. Afternoon free to settle in, walk the pier area, or eat early. On your own this evening.

  2. Day 02

    On the water

    The Coron Ultimate Tour C, full day. Leave the pier early to reach Kayangan Lake before the day boats arrive. Twin Lagoon, Siete Pecados snorkelling, the Skeleton Wreck, CYC Beach, lunch on White Beach. Back to town by mid-afternoon.

  3. Day 03

    Out

    Breakfast at the hotel, then check-out by noon. Free time until the airport transfer. Standard transfer to Busuanga for departure.

Travel times
Busuanga airport to Coron Town: around one hour by van. The Ultimate Tour boat day runs roughly 7am to 4pm. Most lake and lagoon stops involve a short hike or climb from the boat.
When to come
December through May is dry and the boat day runs reliably. June through November is wet season; sea conditions can shift the tour order or, occasionally, cancel a day on the water. The peak months are March through May, which is also when crowds are largest at Kayangan.
Worth knowing
Coron Town is small; everything is walkable. The Coron Ultimate Tour C is a private boat for your group only, not a shared joiner. The lakes involve climbing, Kayangan especially. Swimming is non-negotiable; nearly every stop on Day 2 involves entering the water. We can extend the trip by adding the Reefs and Wrecks tour, a Culion day, or a beach stay at Two Seasons; all arranged after booking, not at checkout.

Three days in Coron is enough for the Ultimate Tour, the airport in and out, and one quiet morning in town. If you want a fuller Palawan return, the Reefs and Wrecks tour or a Culion island day can be added on Day 3, and we can arrange a slower five-day version with a beach stay at Two Seasons on Bulalacao if that’s the trip you’d rather have.
